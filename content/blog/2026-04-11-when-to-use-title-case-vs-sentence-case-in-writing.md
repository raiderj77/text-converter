## Overview of Title Case
------------------------

### Definition

Title Case is a formatting style in which the first word of a sentence, proper nouns, and major words are capitalized. It's commonly used in titles, subtitles, and headings.

### Characteristics

* The first word of each sentence is capitalized.
* Proper nouns (names of people, places, organizations, etc.) are capitalized.
* Major words (nouns, verbs, adjectives, adverbs, etc.) are capitalized.

### Examples

* "The Great Gatsby"
* "Python Programming Language"

## Overview of Sentence Case
-------------------------

### Definition

Sentence Case, also known as lower case, is a formatting style in which only the first word of a sentence is capitalized. It's commonly used in body text, emails, and everyday writing.

### Characteristics

* Only the first word of each sentence is capitalized.
* Proper nouns are not capitalized.
* Minor words (conjunctions, prepositions, articles, etc.) are not capitalized.

### Examples

* "python is a popular programming language."
* "the best way to learn is by doing."

## When to Use Title Case
-------------------------

### Use in Titles and Headings

Title Case is the go-to choice for titles, subtitles, and headings. It's a time-honored convention that grabs attention and makes information scannable.

* **Use in online content**: blogs, articles, social media posts.
* **Use in print content**: books, magazines, reports.

### Use in Special Cases

There are a few situations where Title Case is the preferred choice:

* **Acronyms**: NASA, HTML, GDP.
* **Brand names**: Apple, Google, Facebook.
* **Technical terms**: API, GUI, SQL.

## When to Use Sentence Case
---------------------------

### Use in Body Text

Sentence Case is the preferred choice for body text, as it's easier to read and understand. It's ideal for:

* **Emails**: keep your emails concise and readable.
* **Chatbots**: use Sentence Case for a more conversational tone.
* **Social media**: engage with your audience using Sentence Case.

### Use in Informal Writing

Sentence Case is often used in informal writing, such as:

* **Text messages**: keep your messages concise and readable.
* **Blogs**: use Sentence Case for a more relaxed tone.
* **Email signatures**: sign off with a friendly touch.

## FAQ
----

### Q: Can I use Title Case in body text?
A: While it's technically possible, it's not the most readable choice for body text.

### Q: Can I use Sentence Case in titles?
A: While it's not the standard convention, it's not unheard of either. However, it's generally recommended to stick with Title Case for titles and headings.

### Q: How do I know when to use Title Case or Sentence Case?
A: Ask yourself: what's the purpose of the text? If it's a title or heading, use Title Case. If it's body text, use Sentence Case.
